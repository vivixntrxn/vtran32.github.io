How the Game Works

Pickleball is simple to learn but has a few unique rules that shape how the game unfolds. Here's a quick overview of the court and the core concepts every beginner should know.

Key Concepts

  • Serving Rules

    Start each point by hitting the ball underhand into the oppoiste box.

  • Double Bounce

    After serving, the ball has to bounce once on your opponent's side and once on yours before anyone is allowed to hit it in the air.

  • Kitchen

    This is the area near the net. You can't step inside the kitchen to hit a ball unless it has already bounced.

  • Faults
    • The point ends if the ball goes out, hits the net, or fails the double bounce rule.
    • If a serve lands in the kitchen, it's considered out.
    • If the ball hits a player before it bounces, the play stops and the other team gets the point.

How Scoring Works

Score Call

My Score - Opponent Score - Server Number (1 or 2)

  • When it is your team's turn to serve, the player on the right serves first. They are Server Number 1 for that round, and the player on the left is Server Number 2.
  • You only score points when your team is serving.
  • If your team scores a point, swap sides with your partner.
  • If your team loses the point on Serve Number 1, the serve goes to Server Number 2.
  • If your team loses the point on Server Number 2, the serve goes to the other team.

Common Terms

Serve

The shot that starts each point, hit underhand into the opposite diagonal box.

Return

The shot hit back after the serve. It must bounce before being hit.

Rally

The sequence of shots played back and forth until a fault ends the point.

Drive

A fast and powerful shot hit low over the net to pressure opponents.

Drop

A soft shot that lands in the kitchen, forcing opponents to hit upward.

3rd Shot Drop

The third shot of the rally, usually a soft drop to help your team reach the net.

Reset

A sof shot used to slow down a fast rally and regain control of the point.

Dink

A soft shot hit into the kitchen during net exchanges.

Lob

A high and deep shot hit over opponents to push them back.

Smash

A powerful overhead shot used to finish points.

Topspin

Forward spin applied to the ball to dip it downward and bounce it higher.

Slice

Backspin applied to the ball, making it float and stay low after bouncing.

Volley

Hitting the ball out of the air before it bounces (not allowed in the kitchen).

Punch

A compact volley with little backswing, used to redirect balls at the net.

Roll

A topspin shot that curves downward, often used to attack from the kitchen.

Flick

A quick wrist driven shot, usually off a dink or soft ball.

ATP (Around the Post)

A shot hit around the outside of the net post instead of over the net.

Erne

A volley hit while jumping or running outside the sideline near the net.

Poach

When a player crosses over to take their partner's shot.

Nasty Nelson

Hitting the ball off the opponent at the net during the serve. Rare but legal.

Side Out

When the serving team loses the rally and the opponents now serve.

Baseline

The back boundary line of the court. Serves must be hit from behind this line.

Transition Zone

The area between the baseline and the kitchen.

Paddle Tap

Instead of shaking hands at the end of the game, players tap paddles.
